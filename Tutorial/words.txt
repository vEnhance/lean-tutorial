## introduction

- what is lean?
    - a functional programming language
    - used for “formal verification”
- what is formal verification?
    - you can write mathematical proofs in lean
    - lean will check whether or not the proof is correct
        - won’t “compile” unless it is correct
    - correctness is derived from personal definitions, and definitions/axioms in mathlib
        - someone has to define the natural numbers “correctly” in mathlib
        - lots of choices for how to define things
- for mathematicians, the key resources:
    - mathlib4: actively developed library with many the math primitives
        https://leanprover-community.github.io/mathlib4_docs/index.html
    - mathematics in lean textbook:
        https://leanprover-community.github.io/mathematics_in_lean/
    - lean explore: search for mathlib4 theorems by natural language
        https://leanexplore.com/


## things in lean
- see HYPOTHESES and GOALS in the side panel
- global GOAL is just the theorem statement
- you can create new local GOALS by manually creating/naming hypothesis (think lemma) that you then need to prove
- besides writing intermediate lemmas and defns, your job is to close out goals using TACTICS (rewrite, apply, rcases, etc)
- you can use these hypothesis to close out other goals
- can use `sorry` in place of a valid proof, lean will compile it
